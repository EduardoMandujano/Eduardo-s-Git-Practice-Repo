Eduardo's Pappardelle al Ragu di Cinghiale di Toscana

This recipe requires some planning but it's easy! The night before, marinate the meat. The day the dish will be prepared, prepare the sauce and let it simmer until ready to eat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kilogram of wild boar meat
  • 1 sprig of rosemary
  • 4 sage leaves
  • 5 clove of garlic
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 bottle of dry red wine
  • 2 celery sticks
  • 2 onions
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • 100 grams of pancetta
  • Parsley
  • Calabrian chilies to taste
  • Tomato paste
  • Sea salt and pepper
  • Freshly made pappardelle pasta

Instructions:

  1. In a Ziploc bag add the boar, the rosemary, sage, garlic, bay leaf, cloves, wine, 1 celery, and 1 onion. Seal it, and marinate in the refrigerator.
  2. Remove the boar from the marinade. In a large frying pan, the olive oil. Then, add the boar and marinade.
  3. Reduce until the meat caramelizes.
  4. In a cast-iron skillet, add olive oil, the pancetta and remaining ingredients, except for the pasta.
  5. Cook until soft, then add boar with tomato paste, remaining wine, salt and pepper.
  6. Turn down the heat, and cook for at least 4 hours. Add more wine if the mixture dries out.
  7. Boil water with salt in a large pot, and cook the pappardelle al dente. Strain.
  8. Serve family style on a large platter, top the pappardelle with the sauce.
  9. Enjoy with a glass of Chianti from Toscana.
